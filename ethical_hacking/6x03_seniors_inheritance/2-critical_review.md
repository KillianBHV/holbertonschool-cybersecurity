# Source Material Critical Review

|Finding|First impression|Zone of doubt or confidence|To investigate in Task 3|
|---|---|---|---|
|F-01|The API appears to suffer from a genuine authorization failure allowing cross-tenant access to diagnostic results.|I am confident that the technical issue is real because it was reproduced, but I am not yet convinced that the initial High rating reflects the client's actual exposure.|Determine whether result identifiers can realistically be discovered across laboratories and whether monitoring or tenant isolation limits exploitation.
|F-02|This looks like a classic stored XSS affecting the internal support portal.|I generally trust the rating, although the real impact depends on what support analysts are actually able to access after code execution.|Confirm whether the CSP is enforced, review support-role privileges, and evaluate what actions an attacker could realistically perform.
|F-03|The connector token appears to combine excessive privileges with an unusually long validity period.|I trust the finding itself, but I want to understand whether operational controls compensate for the broad `integration:all` scope.|Review token rotation practices, connector scope design, and network restrictions limiting token misuse.
|F-04|The disabled certificate verification immediately raises concern, the report mentions compensating controls, but I would like to verify whether they are consistently enforced before judging the severity.|I am not fully convinced that the High severity is appropriate until the actual traffic path is confirmed.|Verify whether every connection is forced through the managed mTLS gateway and whether any bypass of that gateway is possible.
|F-05|The webhook lacks message authentication, making status manipulation technically plausible.|I trust the integrity issue, but I do not yet know whether forged status updates produce meaningful operational consequences.|Determine which downstream processes rely on webhook data and whether additional validation prevents unauthorized state changes.
|F-06|The implementation clearly differs from the documented retention policy.|I am confident the discrepancy exists, but I want to verify whether it represents a genuine security issue or an approved operational exception.|Review approved retention requirements, object lifecycle configuration, and regulatory obligations for exported medical data.
|F-07|At first glance this appears extremely severe because a Support Manager can export data from multiple laboratories.|I would not immediately accept the Critical rating because the report describes several approval mechanisms, I would like to confirm that they are mandatory for every production use before accepting the Critical rating.|Confirm that production always enforces DPO approval, dual authorization, WebAuthn, encryption, and audit logging before reassessing severity.
|F-08|The password recovery process leaks account existence through different responses.|I trust the observation, but I am unsure how valuable this information would be to an attacker in MediPath's environment.|Assess MFA adoption, SSO coverage, account sensitivity, and the effectiveness of rate limiting against enumeration attempts.
|F-09|Administrative permissions persist after role downgrade because of delayed authorization updates.|I am confident that the behaviour deserves attention, although I want to understand whether existing governance controls reduce the practical risk.|Verify session revocation mechanisms, cache invalidation behaviour, privileged-action monitoring, and role-change frequency.
|F-10|The analytics service appears to trust tenant identity provided through a request header instead of a verified source.|I trust the technical finding, but its severity depends on who can actually reach the service outside the gateway.|Identify which systems can communicate directly with the analytics service and determine whether authenticated service identities fully protect this trust boundary.|
