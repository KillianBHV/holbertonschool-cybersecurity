# The Handshake Deal
## 1) What happened?
- NorthBay Consulting initiated a network penetration test based solely on **a verbal authorization from the client's CTO**. During the assessment, the consultants exploited a SQL injection vulnerability and extracted a sample of 500 records **to demonstrate impact**. Because CEO and other stakeholders had not been informed of the engagement, the activity was considered as a **real security breach**.
- The lack of valid organizational authorization resulted in legal action, a regulatory investigation, and termination of the engagement.

## 2) Violations
- The engagement did **not follow fundamental pre-engagement requirements** expected in professional penetration testing.
- Testing began without documented written authorization from an individual with the legal authority to approve third-party access to company systems.
- Depending on the applicable jurisdiction, the activities may also have conflicted with legal requirements governing unauthorized access and processing of personal data.

## 3) Professional posture
- NorthBay Consulting should habe required written authorization signed by an individual with the appropriate legal authority before beginning any testing.
- The engagement should also have included **a formal pre-engagement** process defining the scope, stakeholders, communication procedures and notification of *relevant internal departments* such as legal and security operations. 

## 4) Pre-engagement specific clauses
> Prioritary to: **`Rules of Engagement`**, **`Timing of the assessment`**, **`Scopes`**<br>
> But also: **`written authorization contracts`**, **`communication procedures`**
